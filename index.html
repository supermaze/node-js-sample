There are plenty of people who care about injustices that affect them, but only a small handful take the next step and vocalize their beliefs. And usually, by the time people driven to the point of protest, the injustice has gone too far. Luckily, that's a problem that we know how to fix.

We propose to develop a website and mobile app that will allow people to organize protests and discuss issues via an easy-to-use interface. Through this website, users will be able to harness the power of social networking sites to plan gatherings, demonstrations, and protests with the goal of making an impact. With your help, we can change the world.

FAQ

The internet is often unaccessible in regions of the world where protests are common. What do you have to say about the effectiveness of your website in these areas?
We plan to target areas where protests are not occuring, so this is a null issue. The problem we seek to remedy is that of the "armchair activist" - people who are passionate about a movement but for one reason or another neglect to do anything about it offline.
What will this website enable people to do that cannot already be done on facebook, twitter, or any of the other popular social networking sites?
Those websites are great for their intended uses, like connecting with friends or sharing random thoughts. And while their functionality may be so general such that they can be used to organize protests, it is that same generality that prevents people from taking that extra step and making a tangible difference. The specificity of our proposed website combined with layouts tailored for its use will make it a significantly more effective tool.
